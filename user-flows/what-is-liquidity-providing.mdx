## What is a liquidity pool?

A liquidity pool is a smart contract that holds two tokens and lets anyone swap between them at any time. When you swap SOL for USDC on Raydium, you're trading directly against a pool — no order book, no counterparty waiting on the other side. The pool always quotes a price based on the ratio of the two tokens it holds.

A **liquidity provider (LP)** is someone who deposits tokens into one of these pools. Without LPs, there's nothing to trade against.

## What do you actually do as an LP?

You deposit two tokens into a pool in a set ratio. In return you receive a claim on your share of the pool — either a **fungible LP token** (CPMM) or a **position NFT** (CLMM). As long as your capital is in the pool, every swap that goes through it pays a small fee. That fee accumulates and is yours to claim.

When you're ready to exit, you withdraw your share of the pool and receive back the two tokens (plus any earned fees), in whatever ratio the pool currently holds.

## Why do LPs get paid?

You're providing a service: anyone who wants to swap between the two tokens in your pool can do so instantly, at a known price, any time of day. The swap fee — typically 0.01%, 0.25%, or 1% of the trade size depending on the pool — compensates you for that.

The more trading volume flows through the pool, the more fee income LPs collect. High-volume pools on major pairs (SOL/USDC, etc.) can generate meaningful fee APR. Low-volume or very new pairs earn little until they attract traders.

## What are the risks?

### Impermanent loss (IL)

The biggest risk for LPs is **impermanent loss**: when the price of one token moves significantly relative to the other, you end up holding more of the token that declined and less of the token that rose. Compared to simply holding the two tokens in your wallet, you come out behind.

The term "impermanent" is a bit misleading — it only reverses if the price returns to exactly where it was when you deposited. In practice, you should treat IL as a real cost and factor it against the fee income you expect to earn.

- **Low IL risk:** stable/stable pairs (USDC/USDT), liquid staking token pairs (jitoSOL/SOL). Prices barely diverge.
- **High IL risk:** memecoin/SOL, new token launches, anything with high volatility.

See [Impermanent loss math](/algorithms/impermanent-loss) for worked examples.

### Out-of-range risk (CLMM only)

In a concentrated liquidity pool (CLMM), your deposit only earns fees while the market price is inside the range you chose. If the price moves outside your range, your position earns **zero fees** until either the price returns or you rebalance. Out-of-range positions are not a loss by themselves, but you're earning nothing while tied up.

### Smart contract risk

The pool programs are audited, but all on-chain code carries inherent risk. See [Security](/security) for Raydium's audit history and the bug bounty.

### Rug pull / token risk

If one of the two tokens you LP is a scam token or loses all value, your LP position will consist almost entirely of that worthless token by the time you withdraw. Only LP pairs you trust.

## CPMM vs CLMM — which is right for you?

Raydium has two main LP products. The right choice depends on how active you want to be:

| | CPMM (Standard AMM) | CLMM (Concentrated Liquidity) |
|---|---|---|
| **Effort** | Deposit once, forget it | Monitor & rebalance your price range |
| **Fee earnings** | Earn fees at any price | Earn fees only while in range |
| **Capital efficiency** | Standard | 4–40× higher when in range |
| **IL exposure** | Standard full-range IL | Amplified within the range |
| **Best for** | Passive LPs, volatile pairs, new tokens | Active LPs, correlated pairs (stable/stable, LST/SOL) |

**New to LP?** Start with CPMM. It's simpler, you don't need to manage a range, and you earn fees at any price.

## Single-sided entry (CLMM)

In CLMM, if you open a position whose range is entirely **above** the current price, you deposit only token A. If it's entirely **below** the current price, you deposit only token B. This is called single-sided entry and is intentional — it means you're effectively placing a limit order at that range.

This can be useful, but if you open a single-sided position accidentally (by choosing a range that doesn't include the current price), you'll earn no fees right away. The UI warns you before you sign.

## How fees work

- **CPMM:** fees compound directly into the pool reserves. Your LP token automatically represents a growing share of the pool. You don't need to claim anything until you withdraw.
- **CLMM:** fees accrue in your position account and must be **collected explicitly** (Collect fees button in the UI). They don't auto-compound. Collect them when they're worth the transaction cost.
