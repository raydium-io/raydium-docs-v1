## What is a liquidity pool?

A liquidity pool is a smart contract that holds two tokens and lets anyone swap between them at any time. When you swap SOL for USDC on Raydium, you're trading directly against a pool — no order book, no counterparty waiting on the other side. The pool always quotes a price based on the ratio of the two tokens it holds.

A **liquidity provider (LP)** is someone who deposits tokens into one of these pools. Without LPs, there's nothing to trade against.

## What do you do as an LP?

You deposit two tokens into a pool in a set ratio. In return you receive a claim on your share of the pool — either a fungible LP token (CPMM) or a position NFT (CLMM). As long as your capital is in the pool, every swap that goes through it pays a small fee. That fee accumulates and is yours to claim.

When you're ready to exit, you withdraw your share of the pool and receive back the two tokens (plus any earned fees), in whatever ratio the pool currently holds.

## Why do LPs get paid?

You're providing a service: anyone who wants to swap between the two tokens in your pool can do so instantly, at a known price, any time of day. The swap fee — typically 0.01%, 0.25%, or 1% of the trade size depending on the pool — compensates you for that.

The more trading volume flows through the pool, the more fee income LPs collect. High-volume pools on major pairs (SOL/USDC, etc.) can generate meaningful fee APR. Low-volume or very new pairs earn little until they attract traders.

## What about the risks?

LPing isn't free money. The main trade-off is **impermanent loss** — when the two token prices diverge you can end up worse off than if you'd simply held them — alongside out-of-range, token, and smart-contract risks. These are covered in full on [LP risks](/user-flows/lp-risks); read it before your first deposit.
