<Info>
  Single-sided entry is a feature of CLMM, not a bug. When your chosen price range is entirely above or entirely below the current price, the pool only needs one token. This is by design — but it's also easy to trigger by accident.
</Info>

## What single-sided entry is

In a CLMM pool, every position has a lower price and upper price. The current market price determines which tokens are needed to fund the position:

- **Current price is inside your range:** you deposit both tokens in a ratio determined by how close the price is to each edge.
- **Current price is below your lower bound:** you deposit only token A (the base/quote token above the current price). No token B is needed.
- **Current price is above your upper bound:** you deposit only token B. No token A is needed.

This is called **single-sided** or **quote-only** entry, depending on which token is involved.

## Why it happens

**Intentional:** You want to act like a limit order. By placing a range entirely above the current price, you're saying "convert my token B to token A if the price rises into this range." You earn fees while in range, and if the price passes through your range, you exit with the other token.

**Accidental:** You set a range that seems reasonable but doesn't bracket the current price, or you enter prices in the wrong order. The UI warns you with a visible "out of range" or "single-sided" indicator before you sign — read it.

## How single-sided positions behave

When a single-sided CLMM position is fully out of range:

- You hold 100% of one token in the position.
- You earn **zero fees** — there is no in-range liquidity to collect from.
- Your position does not lose money unless price enters your range and then leaves again (creating IL).
- You can still close the position and retrieve your tokens at any time.

When price enters your range from below:

- Your token B begins converting to token A as swaps move through your ticks.
- You start earning fees.
- As price moves further into your range, the ratio shifts toward token A.
- If price passes through your entire range, you end up holding 100% token A plus all accumulated fees.

## Quote-only liquidity

"Quote-only" typically refers to placing a position above the current price using only the quote currency (e.g., USDC in a SOL/USDC pool). This is equivalent to a limit buy: you deposit USDC and it converts to SOL as the price rises into your range.

This is a legitimate LP strategy, particularly for LPs who want to accumulate a base asset at a higher price with fee income along the way.

## CPMM comparison

CPMM does not support single-sided entry. You always deposit both tokens at the current pool ratio. If you want to deposit only one token into a CPMM pool, you would need to swap first, then deposit.

## UI indicators to watch

Before confirming any CLMM deposit, the UI shows:

- **In range / out of range** status.
- **Token ratio** required — if it shows 100% of one token, you are in a single-sided entry.
- **Warning** if the range does not include the current price.

Do not ignore these warnings. An accidental out-of-range deposit earns nothing until price moves.

## Removing a single-sided position

Close or decrease a single-sided position the same way as any CLMM position (see [Managing positions](/user-flows/managing-positions)). You retrieve whichever token(s) the position holds at the time.

If the position is fully out of range, you retrieve 100% of the original deposited token (plus any fees if price briefly passed through the range).
