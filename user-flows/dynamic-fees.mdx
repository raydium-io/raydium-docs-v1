<Info>
  "Dynamic fees" in Raydium's context refers to the pool's fixed fee tier plus a dynamic protocol fee that adjusts over time. For most LPs the relevant number is the **LP fee rate** — the portion of the swap fee that goes to you.
</Info>

## Fee tiers on Raydium

Every pool has a single fee tier set at creation. It cannot be changed afterwards.

### CPMM fee tiers

| Tier | LP receives | Protocol receives | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.01% | ~0.01% | Small portion | Stable pairs |
| 0.25% | ~0.22% | ~0.03% | Standard volatile pairs |
| 1% | ~0.9% | ~0.1% | Long-tail / exotic pairs |

### CLMM fee tiers

| Tier | LP receives | Protocol receives | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.01% | ~0.01% | Portion | Stablecoin/stablecoin |
| 0.05% | ~0.04% | Portion | Correlated pairs |
| 0.25% | ~0.22% | Portion | Volatile majors |
| 1% | ~0.9% | Portion | Long-tail / exotic |

The exact protocol fee split is set by Raydium governance and varies by program version. For current values see [`reference/fee-comparison`](/reference/fee-comparison).

## How fees flow to LPs

**CPMM:** Each swap adds fee tokens directly to the pool reserves. The total `x` and `y` balances grow. Your LP token represents a fixed percentage of the pool, so as reserves grow, your token redeems for more. You do not need to claim — withdraw your LP tokens and you receive principal plus all compounded fees.

**CLMM:** Fees are tracked per-tick and credited to active positions proportionally. They accumulate as a claimable balance on your position NFT. They do not auto-compound. To reinvest, collect manually and re-deposit.

## Fee APR calculation

Approximate annual fee APR for a pool:

```
Fee APR ≈ (24h volume × fee rate × 365) / TVL
```

Example: a $5M TVL pool doing $2M/day at 0.25% fee:

```
(2,000,000 × 0.0025 × 365) / 5,000,000 = 36.5% gross fee APR
```

This is the pool-level APR. Your personal APR equals the pool APR × your share of the pool.

For CLMM, your effective APR is higher when in range (because your capital is concentrated) and zero when out of range. Use [`algorithms/clmm-apr`](/algorithms/clmm-apr) to estimate.

## Picking the right fee tier

The right fee tier matches the pair's volatility and expected trading behaviour.

| Signal | Suggested tier |
|---|---|
| Near-zero price variance (USDC/USDT) | 0.01% |
| Moderate variance, correlated assets | 0.05%–0.25% |
| Significant variance, major tokens | 0.25% |
| High variance or low-liquidity pair | 1% |

**Why not always use 1%?** A high fee discourages traders and reduces volume. Less volume means less fee income despite a higher rate. Check volume-to-TVL across existing fee tiers for the same pair before choosing. The fee tier that maximises `volume × rate` is almost always better than the highest available tier.

**Why not always use 0.01%?** On a volatile pair, IL accumulates faster than 0.01% can recover it. Very low tiers only make sense for pairs that barely move.

## Multiple pools at different fee tiers

Multiple CPMM or CLMM pools can exist for the same token pair at different fee tiers. Raydium's router sends swaps to the pool with the best price, so volume is not guaranteed to go to a specific tier.

In practice, LPs cluster at the tier with the best historical volume. Before creating a pool at a given fee tier, check whether that tier already has a well-funded pool for the pair.

## Protocol fee and governance

A small portion of each swap fee goes to the Raydium protocol treasury. This rate is adjustable by governance. For the current rate and history, see [`reference/fee-comparison`](/reference/fee-comparison) and [`ray/protocol-fees`](/ray/protocol-fees).
