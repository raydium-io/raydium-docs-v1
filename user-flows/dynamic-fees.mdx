<Info>
  CLMM pools have two fee layers: a **base fee tier** fixed at creation, and an optional **dynamic fee** surcharge that increases during high volatility and settles as conditions stabilize. Both go to LPs automatically through the same accumulators.
</Info>

## Fee tiers

A pool's **fee tier** is the percentage taken from every swap that routes through it. It is fixed at pool creation and cannot be changed afterwards. That fee is split between liquidity providers and the protocol — the LP share is what rewards you for supplying liquidity.

Each program offers a range of tiers so creators can match the fee to the pair: low tiers for pairs that barely move (stablecoins, pegged assets), higher tiers for volatile or long-tail pairs where bigger per-trade fees offset the risk. For the available tiers and the exact LP/protocol split, see [`reference/fee-comparison`](/reference/fee-comparison).

## How fees flow to LPs

Most of each swap's fee — the LP share shown above, after a small protocol cut — is distributed to liquidity providers in proportion to their share of the pool's active liquidity. How it reaches you depends on the pool type:

**CPMM:** Each swap adds fee tokens directly to the pool reserves. The total `x` and `y` balances grow. Your LP token represents a fixed percentage of the pool, so as reserves grow, your token redeems for more. You do not need to claim — withdraw your LP tokens and you receive principal plus all compounded fees.

**CLMM:** Fees are tracked per-tick and credited to active positions proportionally. They accumulate as a claimable balance on your position NFT. They do not auto-compound. To reinvest, collect manually and re-deposit.

## Dynamic fees (CLMM)

Dynamic fees add an automatic surcharge on top of a CLMM pool's base fee tier, scaled to recent volatility. This pays LPs more when their risk is highest: sharp price moves are when impermanent loss rises and arbitrageurs pick off positions fastest. The surcharge:

- **During high volatility:** the surcharge rises, compensating LPs for the additional IL risk from sharp price moves.
- **During calm periods:** the surcharge decays back toward zero, and the pool charges only the base tier.
- **Combined rate cap:** the total fee (base + surcharge) cannot exceed 10%.

LPs earn higher fees during volatile periods with no extra action — the surcharge flows through the same accumulators as the base fee, so you collect exactly as you normally would.

Dynamic fees are opt-in at pool creation, and pools that use them are marked in the UI — so standard CLMM pools are unaffected and trade as usual. Like the base fee tier, the choice is **permanent: it can be enabled at creation, but not added, removed, or changed afterward.**

For the full mechanism, see [CLMM fees and rewards](/products/clmm/fees#dynamic-fee).

## Quote-only fees (CLMM)

Quote-only fees settle all of a CLMM pool's swap fees in a single token you choose, regardless of trade direction. By default a pool pays fees in whichever token each swap spends, so an LP accumulates both — including the volatile one. On a pair like MEMECOIN/USDC that leaves income piling up in the memecoin: hard to value in dollars, and unwanted exposure. Settling in the stable or quote asset keeps fee income consistent and dollar-denominated; direction is handled automatically.

Quote-only fees are set when the CLMM pool is created. Like the fee tier and the dynamic-fee setting, the choice is **permanent: it can be enabled at creation, but not changed afterward.**

This option is purely about fee *settlement* — it is unrelated to single-sided *entry* (how a CLMM position is funded).

## Fee Share (CPMM)

**Fee Share** is a configurable creator fee built into Raydium's CPMM program. It lets the deployer behind a pool earn revenue on every swap for the pool's full lifetime — a layer that sits *on top of* the standard swap fee and leaves the LP fee untouched.

- **Set at pool creation.** Fee Share is configured when the pool is launched, and applies to every swap that follows.
- **Paid in any asset.** Earnings accrue on-chain and are collectable on demand, settled in SOL, USDC, or a token of choice.
- **Permissioned.** Fee Share pools can only be issued through approved platforms, making it a B2B infrastructure layer. The deployer behind each pool sets the rate and decides where the revenue goes.

Revenue scales linearly with volume. At \$100M of cumulative swap volume:

| Fee Share rate | Platform revenue |
|---|---|
| 10 bps (0.1%) | \$100K |
| 25 bps (0.25%) | \$250K |
| 50 bps (0.5%) | \$500K |
| 100 bps (1%) | \$1M |

This lets deployers on platforms that originate trading demand keep earning as a token's liquidity migrates to Raydium, extending their revenue through the token's full lifecycle. Metaplex, Bonk.fun, and Trends.fun are among the first platforms to use it.

Fee Share is permissioned, so you set it up directly with the Raydium team. Interested in supporting Fee Share for your platform? Email [collab@raydium.io](mailto:collab@raydium.io) to get started.

## Picking the right fee tier

The right fee tier matches the pair's volatility and expected trading behaviour.

| Signal | Suggested tier |
|---|---|
| Near-zero price variance (USDC/USDT) | 0.01% |
| Moderate variance, correlated assets | 0.05%–0.25% |
| Significant variance, major tokens | 0.25% |
| High variance or low-liquidity pair | 1% |

**Why not always use 1%?** A high fee discourages traders and reduces volume. Less volume means less fee income despite a higher rate. Check volume-to-TVL across existing fee tiers for the same pair before choosing. The fee tier that maximises `volume × rate` is almost always better than the highest available tier.

**Why not always use 0.01%?** On a volatile pair, IL accumulates faster than 0.01% can recover it. Very low tiers only make sense for pairs that barely move.
