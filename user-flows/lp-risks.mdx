## Impermanent loss (IL)

Impermanent loss is the most common and most misunderstood LP risk. It occurs whenever the price of one token moves relative to the other after you deposit, and it applies to **every** pool type — CPMM and CLMM alike.

When you deposit into a constant-product (CPMM) pool, the pool rebalances automatically as prices change: it sells the appreciating token and buys the depreciating one to maintain the `x × y = k` invariant. When you withdraw, you receive the current pool ratio — tilted toward the token that fell in price.

A CLMM position experiences the same loss, only **amplified**: concentrating liquidity into a price range makes your capital behave like a leveraged constant-product position, so a given price move produces more IL per dollar than a full-range CPMM position would. The trade-off is that once price exits your range, the IL stops growing (see [Out-of-range risk](#out-of-range-risk-clmm-only) below).

**Example (CPMM full range).** You deposit 1 SOL and 100 USDC when SOL is \$100.

| SOL price at exit | LP value | Hold value | IL |
|---|---|---|---|
| \$100 (unchanged) | \$200 | \$200 | 0% |
| \$150 | \$244.9 | \$250 | −2.0% |
| \$200 | \$282.8 | \$300 | −5.7% |
| \$50 | \$141.4 | \$150 | −5.7% |
| \$400 | \$400 | \$500 | −20% |

IL is symmetric: a 4× move up hurts as much as a 4× move down. The higher the divergence, the worse the IL.

The word "impermanent" means if the price returns to the exact entry price, IL disappears. In practice, don't count on that. Treat IL as a real, expected cost.

### Mitigation

- Choose pairs with correlated price movements (stable/stable, LST/SOL). Correlation shrinks divergence.
- Use a fee tier high enough that swap fee income exceeds expected IL over your holding period.
- Accept that IL is the price of earning fees. Size positions accordingly.

Full IL math: [impermanent loss](/algorithms/impermanent-loss).

## Out-of-range risk (CLMM only)

In a CLMM position, you pick a price range. Your liquidity only earns fees while the current price is inside that range. When price leaves the range:

- You earn **zero fees**.
- You hold 100% of one token (the one the price moved toward).
- IL is locked in at the range boundary — it doesn't worsen further unless price re-enters the range and exits again.

Out-of-range positions do not lose money in isolation — but they stop working. If you don't notice for weeks, you could have earned nothing while tying up capital.

**CLMM tight ranges amplify both fee income and out-of-range frequency.** A ±2% range around SOL/USDC captures a lot of fees when in range, but SOL moves more than 2% regularly. Rebalance or use a wider range.

## Token / rug risk

If one of the two tokens in your LP pair loses most of its value:

- The pool will automatically accumulate more of the worthless token and sell your other token against it.
- On withdrawal, you get almost entirely the worthless token back.
- LP fees don't compensate for a −95% price move in one side.

**Only LP pairs you trust.** For new or unaudited tokens, consider the LP position equivalent to a leveraged directional bet on that token.

## Smart contract risk

All Raydium pool programs have been audited (see [Security](/security)). But audits are not guarantees. Any on-chain program can contain bugs, and Solana's account model creates edge cases. The risk is low compared to unaudited protocols, but it is not zero.

Do not LP an amount you are not willing to lose entirely.

## Summary table

| Risk | Applies to | How to reduce |
|---|---|---|
| Impermanent loss | All pools (amplified on CLMM) | Choose correlated pairs, appropriate fee tier |
| Out-of-range | CLMM only | Widen range, rebalance regularly |
| Token/rug | All pools | Only LP trusted tokens |
| Smart contract | All pools | Stick to audited programs |
