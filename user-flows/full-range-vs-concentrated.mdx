## What "range" means in CLMM

Every CLMM position has a lower price bound and an upper price bound. Your liquidity is only active — only earns fees — while the current market price is between those two bounds.

The narrower your range, the more concentrated your capital, the higher your fee share per dollar deposited, and the more likely you are to go out of range.

The wider your range, the lower your concentration, the lower your fee APR, but the longer you can leave the position alone.

## Full-range positions

A full-range CLMM position sets the lower bound to the minimum possible price and the upper bound to the maximum. It behaves almost identically to a CPMM deposit:

- Capital is spread across all prices.
- It never goes out of range.
- Capital efficiency is low — similar to CPMM.
- No monitoring or rebalancing required.

**Why use full-range CLMM instead of CPMM?**
The main use case is [Burn & Earn](/user-flows/burn-and-earn) — permanently locking a CLMM position. CPMM is usually simpler and preferred for plain passive LP. Full-range CLMM is not meaningfully better than CPMM for an ordinary deposit.

## Concentrated positions

Choosing a tight range boosts your effective capital:

```
Capital efficiency ≈ sqrt(P_upper / P_lower) / (sqrt(P_upper / P_lower) - 1)
```

In plain terms, a ±10% range (0.9 to 1.1 of current price) gives roughly 20× capital efficiency vs full range. A ±50% range gives about 4×.

**Example:** You have $10,000 and the SOL/USDC pool is at $160.

| Range | Effective capital | Approx APR multiplier vs CPMM |
|---|---|---|
| Full range | $10,000 | ~1× |
| $80–$320 (±100%) | ~$15,000 | ~1.5× |
| $120–$213 (±33%) | ~$35,000 | ~3.5× |
| $144–$178 (±10%) | ~$100,000 | ~10× |
| $152–$168 (±5%) | ~$200,000 | ~20× |

The multiplier is only realised while price stays in range. If price leaves, you earn nothing — at any range width.

## Choosing a range in practice

### For stable/stable pairs (USDC/USDT)

Use the tightest range that covers historical variance — typically ±0.1%–0.3%. This pair almost never moves outside a very narrow band. Tight ranges here give enormous capital efficiency with very low out-of-range risk.

### For correlated pairs (jitoSOL/SOL, mSOL/SOL)

Use ±2%–5%. These pairs drift slowly as staking yield accrues, but rarely diverge sharply. Rebalance weekly or when either bound is approached.

### For volatile majors (SOL/USDC, ETH/USDC)

Use ±15%–40% depending on your rebalancing tolerance:
- **±15%:** Rebalance every few days to a week during normal volatility.
- **±30%:** Rebalance monthly; survives moderate market moves without going out of range.
- **±50%:** Low concentration, minimal rebalancing; fee APR closer to CPMM.

### For long-tail / memecoin pairs

Full range or very wide CLMM. Tight ranges on highly volatile pairs spend most of their life out of range. CPMM is often better for these pairs.

## Out-of-range behaviour

When price exits your range:

- **Below lower bound:** your position is now 100% token A. No fees earned.
- **Above upper bound:** your position is now 100% token B. No fees earned.
- The position is not lost — you can withdraw or wait for price to return.
- IL is locked at the boundary price; it does not worsen further unless price re-enters the range.

## Rebalancing

When price approaches or exits a range boundary, you have three options:

1. **Wait.** If you expect price to return, doing nothing preserves your position.
2. **Widen the range.** Add liquidity to a new, wider position. This may require increasing or closing and reopening.
3. **Rebalance.** Close the current position, swap tokens to the new ratio, and open a new position centred on the current price.

There is no in-place "move range" instruction. Rebalancing always means closing + reopening.

## IL profile by range width

Concentrated positions have amplified IL. Within a ±10% range, a price move of 5% causes significantly more IL per dollar than in CPMM. The concentration that earns more fees also loses more when price moves.

Wider ranges have lower amplification and approach CPMM IL as you approach full range.
