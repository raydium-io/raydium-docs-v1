<Info>
  Creating a pool is how a token becomes tradable on Raydium. You fund the first liquidity, set the pool's permanent parameters, and earn a share of every swap that flows through it. This page is about *whether* to create one; to choose between pool types, see [CPMM vs CLMM](/user-flows/cpmm-vs-clmm).
</Info>

## Make a token tradable

Until a token has a pool, no one can swap it on Raydium. Creating one bootstraps a market: it opens the token to swaps, makes it discoverable in the pool list, and lets Raydium's router quote it — including multi-hop and split routes — once it has meaningful liquidity. New tokens usually launch as a [CPMM pool](/user-flows/create-cpmm-pool) (the default) or graduate into one automatically from [LaunchLab](/user-flows/launchlab-overview). For a new or illiquid token, this is the entry point to the entire ecosystem.

## Earn on the volume you attract

As the creator you are also the first liquidity provider, so you earn swap fees from day one. The deeper and better-priced your liquidity, the more volume the pool attracts — and the more fees it generates. You can also layer on a [farm](/user-flows/create-farm) to draw in other LPs, or use [Burn & Earn](/user-flows/burn-and-earn) to lock liquidity permanently while still collecting its fees. Platforms that originate trading demand can go further with [Fee Share](/user-flows/dynamic-fees#fee-share-creator-fee) — a creator fee on every swap for the life of the pool (see below).

## Control the launch parameters

The creator sets the pool's permanent terms — pool type, fee tier, and initial price. Establishing the canonical, well-funded pool for a pair means volume and routing concentrate there rather than on a fragmented alternative. For a project launching its own token, this control over the initial market matters.

## Support a token or project

Teams create pools to provide a reliable venue for their community to trade, to pair their token against SOL or USDC, and to coordinate incentives. A well-seeded pool signals commitment and gives holders somewhere dependable to enter and exit.

## For platforms and launchpads

If you run a launchpad, wallet, or app that sends meaningful volume to Raydium, creating pools can be a revenue stream rather than just a way to make a token tradable. Through [Fee Share](/user-flows/dynamic-fees#fee-share-creator-fee), approved platforms earn a configurable creator fee on every swap their pools generate — for the life of the pool, paid in the asset of their choice — on top of the standard swap fee and without touching LP rewards.

Fee Share is permissioned and set up directly with the Raydium team. Reach out at [collab@raydium.io](mailto:collab@raydium.io) to enable Fee Share, or if your platform already drives significant trading volume, and we'll set up your fee structure.

## When not to create a pool

Creating a pool isn't always the right move:

- **A well-funded pool already exists for the pair.** Adding a competing pool at a different fee tier fragments liquidity, and neither pool may end up deep enough to attract volume. Add to the existing pool instead.
- **You can't seed meaningful liquidity.** Underfunded pools give poor pricing and bad UX, and rarely attract traders.
- **You only want exposure, not a market.** If your goal is simply to earn yield, providing liquidity to an existing pool is simpler than creating one.

## Before you start

The pool type, fee tier, and initial price are **permanent** — set at creation and not changeable afterward. Before opening the creation UI, have ready:

- [ ] Both token mint addresses.
- [ ] Enough of each token to seed initial liquidity. Underfunded pools give poor price impact and bad UX; for a CPMM, ~\$5,000–\$10,000 equivalent reduces early price impact.
- [ ] 0.2–0.4 SOL for creation rent, token-account creation, and priority fees (CLMM costs slightly more than CPMM).
- [ ] The current market price of the pair. For CPMM, price is set by your deposit ratio; for CLMM you enter it explicitly. A wrong initial price lets arbitrageurs drain the underpriced token at your expense.

## What happens after creation

1. The pool goes live on-chain immediately and is permissionless — you cannot stop others from trading, adding liquidity, or creating competing pools at other fee tiers.
2. The Raydium UI indexes it within a few minutes; it appears in search and the pool list.
3. Routing starts quoting swaps through the pool once it has meaningful liquidity.
4. Other LPs can add liquidity permissionlessly.
5. You can optionally [create a farm](/user-flows/create-farm) to incentivize LPs with token rewards.
