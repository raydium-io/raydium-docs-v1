<Info>
  **CPMM** positions use fungible LP tokens — simple to track. **CLMM** positions use position NFTs with a selected price range — they need more active attention. Both live on the Portfolio page.
</Info>

## Finding your positions

All open positions are visible at [raydium.io/portfolio](https://raydium.io/portfolio).

- **CPMM positions** show as fungible LP token balances with their underlying token pair and value.
- **CLMM positions** show as position NFTs with their price range, current status (in range / out of range), and accrued fees.
- **Farm positions** show staked LP balances alongside pending reward amounts.

CLMM position NFTs also appear in any Solana wallet that surfaces NFTs.

## Add liquidity to a CPMM pool

1. Open the pool page on Raydium.
2. Click **Add liquidity**.
3. Enter the amount of one token. The other auto-fills at the pool's current ratio.
4. Review the LP tokens you will receive and your expected pool share.
5. Confirm and approve in your wallet.

## Remove liquidity from a CPMM pool

1. Open the pool page or your portfolio position.
2. Click **Remove liquidity**.
3. Enter the LP token amount, or use the percentage slider.
4. Review the two token amounts you will receive.
5. Confirm and approve in your wallet.

Fees compound into LP token value automatically — you receive them as part of the withdrawal.

## Open a CLMM position

1. Open the CLMM pool page.
2. Choose a lower price and upper price.
3. Enter the deposit amount.
4. Review whether the position is in range or out of range.
5. Confirm and approve.

Your range determines the token ratio required. If the current price sits outside your range, the deposit will be single-sided. The UI warns you before you sign.

## Increase, decrease, or close a CLMM position

- **Increase:** open the position card, click **Increase**, enter an amount, and sign. This adds depth without changing your range.
- **Decrease:** click **Decrease**, choose a percentage or amount, review the estimated token return, and sign.
- **Close:** decrease liquidity to zero, collect any pending fees and rewards, then close the position. Closing burns the NFT and returns rent to your wallet.

## Rebalance a CLMM position

There is no in-place range adjustment. When price drifts to the edge of your range, your options are to **wait** (valid if you expect a reversion) or to **rebalance**: close → swap → reopen.

1. **Close** the position (decrease liquidity to zero) and collect any pending fees in the same transaction if prompted.
2. Check your wallet — you now hold the two tokens at whatever ratio the position exited at.
3. **Swap** to rebalance if needed (e.g., you exited mostly in USDC after SOL fell; swap some back to SOL at the new price).
4. **Open** a new position centred on the current price with your desired range.

Only rebalance when the extra fees you expect to capture outweigh the swap and transaction costs.

## Collect CLMM fees

CLMM swap fees accrue on the position as a pending balance and do **not** auto-compound — you collect them manually.

1. Open your CLMM position from the portfolio.
2. Click **Collect fees** (or **Collect all** to take fees and any reward streams together).
3. Review the token amounts.
4. Confirm and approve.

To compound, collect the fees, rebalance the two tokens if needed, then increase liquidity on the position.

## Collect CLMM rewards

Some CLMM pools have reward streams in addition to swap fees.

1. Open the CLMM position.
2. Click **Collect rewards** (or **Collect all**).
3. Review the reward mints and amounts.
4. Confirm and approve.

## Harvest farm rewards

Farm rewards accrue while your CPMM LP tokens are staked in an active farm. Harvesting transfers pending rewards to your wallet without moving your stake.

1. Open your farm position from the portfolio.
2. Click **Harvest**.
3. Review pending amounts per reward stream.
4. Confirm and approve.

Farm deposits and withdrawals often settle pending rewards in the same transaction — if you add or withdraw stake, review the confirmation, as it may also harvest.

## Timing and troubleshooting claims

Every claim is an on-chain transaction and may include a priority fee during congestion.

- For small pending amounts, wait until they're worth more than the transaction fee. Weekly or monthly is enough for most positions; there's no need to claim daily unless you're actively compounding.
- **First claim fails:** refresh the page and retry — a recently created or migrated position may need to initialise account data first.
- **Claim stuck or expired:** retry with a higher priority-fee setting.
- **Transfer-fee reward mints:** if a reward mint charges transfer fees, your net received amount can be lower than the pending display. Check wallet balance changes after confirming.
- **Batch claims fail:** claim a few positions at a time rather than many at once.

After any claim, confirm the transaction status is **Success** and that the token balance increased in your wallet.

## Monitoring

Key things to watch on the portfolio page:

| Metric | What to do |
|---|---|
| CLMM "out of range" | Consider rebalancing |
| Low pending fees (CLMM) | No action needed; collect when worthwhile |
| Farm reward APR falling | Normal as more LPs stake; check total return still meets your target |
| One-sided token balance (CLMM) | Price has moved significantly; decide to rebalance or wait |
