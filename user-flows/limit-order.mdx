<Info>
  A limit order rests until the market reaches a price you set, then fills automatically — you don't need to be online for it to execute. Use it when you have a target entry or exit price and would rather wait for it than take the current one. For an immediate swap at the current price, see [Market order](/user-flows/market-order).
</Info>

## How limit orders work

Limit orders are a CLMM feature. Your order is parked at a single price (one tick) on a CLMM pool — when several pools exist for the pair, it's placed on the one with the highest TVL. When a later swap moves the market through that price, your order is filled first, at your exact limit price, before the trade reaches the pool's curve, and the output is pushed to your wallet (an off-chain keeper settles it for you).

Mechanically, a limit order is the same as opening a single-sided CLMM position whose range sits entirely on one side of the current price — though unlike a regular position it only fills one way and won't convert back if the price returns.

## Placing one

1. On the [Swap page](/user-flows/swap), flip the **Market / Limit** switcher to **Limit**.
2. Choose the pair, set your target price and the amount. The price has to sit on the side the order can fill from: a **sell** must be priced **above** the current pool price, and a **buy** must be priced **below** it.
3. Confirm and sign. Your input tokens are escrowed at that price until the order fills or you cancel.

## How it fills

- The order rests at your chosen price until a trade crosses it. Orders at the same price fill **first-in, first-out**.
- A large enough cross fills your order completely; a smaller one fills it partially and leaves the rest resting.
- Because you fill at your exact price ahead of the curve, you act as the maker — that portion of the swap pays no LP fee, and the swapper gets a small price improvement.

## Managing an order

- **Add** to an open order, **reduce or cancel** it (you receive the unfilled remainder plus anything already filled), or **close** a fully filled order to reclaim its rent.
- Until it fills, your tokens remain yours — cancelling returns them.
