<Info>
  Swapping trades one token for another against Raydium's liquidity pools. Every swap is one of two order types, chosen with the **Market / Limit** toggle at the top of the swap box.
</Info>

## What swapping is

A swap trades one token directly for another, straight from your wallet. You connect, pick the token you're paying and the token you want, review the price, and sign — the tokens are exchanged against Raydium's pooled liquidity, with no counterparty, order book, or sign-up needed.

Key points:

- **Any token with a pool:** swap majors, stablecoins, and long-tail or newly launched tokens alike.
- **Automatic best price:** Raydium's router compares every eligible pool and routes your trade for the most output.
- **Two order types:** take the current price now (market) or set a target and wait (limit).
- **Self-custody:** you sign from your own wallet; nothing moves until the swap executes.
- **Fast and cheap:** swaps settle in seconds on Solana with low network fees.

## Order types

Raydium supports two ways to execute a swap, switched with the **Market / Limit** toggle at the top of the swap box:

- **[Market order](/user-flows/market-order)** — fills immediately at the current best price. The default, and the most common way to trade. Start here if you just want to swap now.
- **[Limit order](/user-flows/limit-order)** — rests until the market reaches a price you set, then fills automatically. Use it when you have a target price and would rather wait for it.

## How a swap routes

When several pools exist for a pair, you don't pick which one to trade against — the router does it for you. It quotes each eligible pool and chooses the best execution: a single pool, a multi-hop path through an intermediate token, or a split across pools to reduce price impact. CPMM, CLMM, and the legacy AMM v4 pools are all eligible, and the UI shows the chosen route before you sign.
