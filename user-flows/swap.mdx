<Info>
  Swapping is the most common operation on Raydium: you trade one token for another against the protocol's liquidity pools. Every swap is one of two order types, chosen with the **Market / Limit** toggle at the top of the swap box.
</Info>

## How a swap works

You pick the token you're paying and the token you want, and Raydium's router quotes the best execution across every eligible pool — CPMM, CLMM, and the legacy AMM v4. Depending on liquidity, it may route through a single pool, hop through an intermediate token, or split the trade across several pools to reduce price impact. You review the quote, set your slippage and priority fee, sign, and the swap settles in a few seconds.

## Order types

Raydium supports two ways to execute a swap:

- **[Market order](/user-flows/market-order)** — fills immediately at the current best price. The default, and the most common way to trade. This is where to start if you just want to swap now.
- **[Limit order](/user-flows/limit-order)** — rests until the market reaches a price you set, then fills automatically. Use it when you have a target price and would rather wait for it than take the current one.

Switch between the two with the **Market / Limit** toggle at the top of the swap box.
