<Info>
  LPing is not free money. It trades price exposure for yield. This page helps you decide whether that trade makes sense for a given pair and position size.
</Info>

## The two income streams

Liquidity providers earn from two sources:

**Swap fees.** Every trade that routes through your pool pays a fee — typically 0.01%–1% of the swap size depending on the pool's fee tier. This accumulates continuously. In CPMM, fees compound automatically into the pool reserves (your LP token value grows). In CLMM, fees accumulate as a claimable balance you collect manually.

**Farm rewards.** Some pools have an additional incentive layer funded by project treasuries or Raydium itself. LPs stake their CPMM LP tokens into a farm and receive token emissions on top of swap fees, proportional to their share of the staked liquidity — so a high launch APR falls as more LPs join. Farm rewards are finite: they run for a fixed schedule and can end.

## When LP income beats just holding

LPing outperforms holding when:

- **Fee APR > impermanent loss.** Impermanent loss (IL) is the value an LP gives up relative to simply holding the two tokens when their prices diverge ([covered in full on LP risks](/user-flows/lp-risks)). Swap fees compensate for that divergence; whether they fully offset it depends on trading volume relative to pool TVL and how far the two tokens' prices move.
- **The pair is correlated.** For pairs that naturally move together — stable/stable, LST/SOL, wrapped variants — IL is low and fee income can make LPing clearly positive.
- **The pool has genuine volume.** A pool with no traders earns no fees. Check 24-hour volume and TVL before depositing. A \$10M TVL pool doing \$1M/day at 0.25% earns roughly 9% APR distributed across LPs.

LPing is likely to underperform holding when:

- You LP a volatile/volatile pair (e.g., memecoin/SOL) with low volume. Price divergence will outpace fee income.
- You use a CLMM tight range but don't rebalance. Your position goes out of range, earns nothing, but still carries the IL from where it went out.
- You chase high farm APR without checking when rewards end. A 300% APR farm running for two more weeks is not what it looks like.

## Common LP approaches

There is no single "LP return" — it depends entirely on the approach you take and the pair you choose. A few common ones:

| Approach | Example pool | What it looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Stable pair** (USDC/USDT) | CLMM 0.01% tight range | Steady, modest fees — the closest thing to "set and earn." |
| **Correlated pair** (jitoSOL/SOL) | CLMM 0.05% medium range | Solid fees with light monitoring. |
| **Major volatile, passive** (SOL/USDC) | CPMM 0.25% | Earns at any price, no management. |
| **Major volatile, active** (SOL/USDC) | CLMM 0.25% range | Top fee capture while in range, but you rebalance often and IL is amplified. |
| **Long-tail / new token** | CPMM 1% | High headline fees, but closer to a directional bet on the token. |

Returns scale with trading volume, your share of the pool, time-in-range (for CLMM), and how far prices move. Treat any APR figure you see as a snapshot, not a promise.

Versus simply holding: holding keeps your full upside but earns nothing, while LPing trades some of that upside (IL) for fee income. On a correlated pair with steady volume, that's often the best risk-adjusted yield on-chain; on a speculative pair, fees rarely cover the IL.
