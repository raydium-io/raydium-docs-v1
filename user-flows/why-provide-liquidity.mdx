<Info>
  LPing is not free money. It trades price exposure for yield. This page helps you decide whether that trade makes sense for a given pair and position size.
</Info>

## The two income streams

Liquidity providers earn from two sources:

**Swap fees.** Every trade that routes through your pool pays a fee — typically 0.01%–1% of the swap size depending on the pool's fee tier. This accumulates continuously. In CPMM, fees compound automatically into the pool reserves (your LP token value grows). In CLMM, fees accumulate as a claimable balance you collect manually.

**Farm rewards.** Some pools have an additional incentive layer funded by project treasuries or Raydium itself. LPs stake their CPMM LP tokens into a farm and receive token emissions on top of swap fees. Farm rewards are finite — they run for a fixed schedule and can end.

## When LP income beats just holding

LPing outperforms holding when:

- **Fee APR > impermanent loss.** Swap fees compensate for price divergence between the two tokens. This depends on trading volume relative to pool TVL and the price movement of the two tokens.
- **The pair is correlated.** For pairs that naturally move together — stable/stable, LST/SOL, wrapped variants — IL is low and fee income can make LPing clearly positive.
- **The pool has genuine volume.** A pool with no traders earns no fees. Check 24-hour volume and TVL before depositing. A $10M TVL pool doing $1M/day at 0.25% earns roughly 9% APR distributed across LPs.

LPing is likely to underperform holding when:

- You LP a volatile/volatile pair (e.g., memecoin/SOL) with low volume. Price divergence will outpace fee income.
- You use a CLMM tight range but don't rebalance. Your position goes out of range, earns nothing, but still carries the IL from where it went out.
- You chase high farm APR without checking when rewards end. A 300% APR farm running for two more weeks is not what it looks like.

## Realistic return ranges

These are illustrative, not guaranteed:

| Pair type | Pool | Expected fee APR (rough) |
|---|---|---|
| Stable/stable (USDC/USDT) | CLMM 0.01% tight range | 15–40% |
| Correlated (jitoSOL/SOL) | CLMM 0.05% medium range | 20–60% |
| Major volatile (SOL/USDC) | CPMM 0.25% | 20–50% |
| Major volatile (SOL/USDC) | CLMM 0.25% active range | 50–150% when in range |
| Long-tail / new token | CPMM 1% | Variable; often 5–30% net of IL |

Actual returns depend on volume, your TVL share, time-in-range (CLMM), and price movements.

## The break-even question

Before depositing, ask: *at what fee APR do I break even against expected IL?*

For a SOL/USDC pool and a 20% SOL price move over your holding period, IL is approximately 1.9% of principal. That's a low bar — a 2% fee APR over the same period covers it. Most meaningful pools clear that bar.

For a volatile memecoin that 10× or 0.1× in price, IL exceeds 50%. No fee APR covers that unless the pool is doing extraordinary volume.

See [LP risks](/user-flows/lp-risks) for the full math and [impermanent loss](/algorithms/impermanent-loss) for worked examples.

## LPing vs yield farming vs staking

| Option | Return source | Risk | Effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| LP (CPMM) | Swap fees + optional farm | Impermanent loss, token risk | Low |
| LP (CLMM active range) | Swap fees | Amplified IL if not rebalanced | Medium-High |
| Farm only (e.g., staking RAY) | Emission rewards | Dilution risk, lock-up | Low |
| Hold | Price appreciation | Full price exposure | None |

LPing on a correlated pair with stable volume is often the best risk-adjusted yield on-chain. LPing on a speculative pair without checking IL is one of the easiest ways to quietly lose money while thinking you're earning.
