<Info>
  Once a pool is created, it is permissionless. You cannot restrict trading, close it while other LPs have funds in it, or change its fee tier. Your controls are limited to your own liquidity and, if you deployed a farm, the farm's reward schedule.
</Info>

## Finding your pool

### By pool ID

Every pool has a unique on-chain address. After creation, the Raydium UI shows the pool address in the confirmation screen. Save it.

To find the pool later:
- Visit [raydium.io/liquidity](https://raydium.io/liquidity) and search by pool ID.
- Or search by the token pair — both mints — in the pool list.

### Via portfolio

Go to [raydium.io/portfolio](https://raydium.io/portfolio). Your LP position in the pool you created appears here alongside all your other positions. This view shows your share of the pool, the current value, and accrued fees or rewards.

### Via Solana explorer

Paste the pool address into a Solana explorer (e.g. solscan.io or explorer.solana.com) to see the raw on-chain state, total reserves, and transaction history.

## What you can monitor

| Metric | Where to find it |
|---|---|
| TVL | Raydium pool page or API |
| 24h volume | Raydium pool page |
| Fee APR | Raydium pool page |
| Your LP share | Portfolio page |
| Your accrued fees | Portfolio page (CPMM: in LP value; CLMM: pending fees) |
| Farm TVL and APR | Farm page or portfolio |
| `burnPercent` | `api-v3.raydium.io/pools/info/list` response |

The `burnPercent` field is useful for showing how much of a pool's liquidity has been permanently locked via [Burn & Earn](/user-flows/burn-and-earn).

## What you can control

### Your own liquidity

Add or remove your LP share at any time. See [Add or remove liquidity](/user-flows/add-remove-liquidity).

### Farm schedule (if you created a farm)

If you deployed a [Farm](/user-flows/create-farm) for the pool, you can:
- Monitor reward emissions and staker count.
- Top up the reward vault before it runs dry.
- Extend the schedule if the UI supports it.
- Reclaim unused rewards after the schedule ends.

You cannot change the farm's staking mint or fee tier.

### Burn & Earn positions

If you locked LP via [Burn & Earn](/user-flows/burn-and-earn), you can collect fees using the Fee Key NFT at any time.

## What you cannot control

- **Fee tier:** permanent at creation. Cannot be changed.
- **Pool type:** permanent. A CPMM pool cannot become a CLMM pool.
- **Other LPs:** anyone can add or remove their own liquidity. You cannot block it.
- **Trading:** anyone can swap through the pool. There is no pause or restrict function.
- **Pool closure:** the pool program account persists on-chain indefinitely. It cannot be closed while any LP tokens remain outstanding.

## Communicating about your pool

As the pool creator, you are the natural point of contact for LPs and traders relying on your pool. Best practices:

- Publish the pool ID and LP mint when you launch.
- Announce any farm schedule changes (top-ups, extensions, wind-downs) before they happen.
- If you plan to migrate liquidity to a new pool (e.g., AMM v4 → CPMM), give LPs at least 48 hours notice and publish the destination pool ID.
- When deprecating a pool, don't just remove your liquidity silently — the pool continues to exist on-chain and may still show in aggregators.

## Deprecating a pool

You cannot delete a pool. To effectively deprecate one:

1. Remove your own liquidity.
2. Wind down or stop topping up any farms.
3. Announce to the community that the pool is no longer supported.
4. Direct traders and LPs to the replacement pool.

The old pool stays on-chain. Other LPs can still withdraw their funds. Aggregators will stop routing to it once liquidity falls below a meaningful threshold.
