## CPMM: set and mostly forget

CPMM positions require little active management:

- Fees auto-compound into LP token value.
- There is no price range to monitor.
- No action is needed unless you want to exit or add more.

The main decision points for CPMM are:
- **Exit triggers:** has the LP thesis changed? (Token turned risky, volume dried up, better opportunity elsewhere.)
- **Size management:** add more as conviction grows, reduce as risk appetite shifts.
- **Farm timing:** harvest farm rewards on a schedule that makes economic sense relative to transaction fees.

## CLMM: active management model

CLMM positions reward attention. The core loop is:

1. **Monitor range status.** Check weekly or more frequently on volatile pairs.
2. **Collect fees.** Collect when accrued fees exceed transaction cost (weekly/monthly for most positions).
3. **Rebalance if needed.** When price approaches a range boundary, decide: wait, or rebalance to a new centred range.
4. **Compound if desired.** Collect fees → rebalance token ratio → increase position.

### When to rebalance

Rebalancing makes sense when:

- Price is within ~10–15% of your range boundary and you expect the trend to continue.
- You have been out of range for more than a few days and don't expect a quick reversal.
- Your position is 100% in one token (fully out of range) and earning nothing.

Rebalancing does **not** make sense when:
- Price briefly exited range and is back inside.
- Transaction costs would exceed the marginal fee improvement.
- You are waiting for a specific price to return.

### Rebalancing cost

Every rebalance requires at least two transactions (close + reopen) plus one or more swaps to rebalance the token ratio. Total cost on Solana is typically $0.01–$0.10 in transaction fees.

For small positions (< $500), frequent rebalancing can consume a meaningful fraction of fee income. Calculate break-even rebalance frequency:

```
Break-even days = (rebalance cost in $) / (daily fee income when in range)
```

If your daily fee income is $0.50 and a rebalance costs $0.05, rebalancing more than once every 10 days is likely counterproductive.

## Range strategy by pair type

| Pair | Range width | Rebalance trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Stable/stable | ±0.1–0.5% | Price within 0.05% of a bound |
| Correlated (LST/SOL) | ±3–8% | Price within 1% of a bound |
| Major volatile (SOL/USDC) | ±15–40% | Price within 5% of a bound |
| Long-tail token | Full range or CPMM | N/A |

Widen ranges as volatility increases. Narrow ranges earn more but break faster. Choose the width you can monitor, not the width that maximises theoretical APR.

## Compounding fees back into a position

CLMM fees do not auto-compound. Manual compounding steps:

1. **Collect fees** from the position.
2. **Check the token ratio.** Your position at current price requires some ratio of token A and B. If you collected mostly token A (because you earned from A-heavy swaps), you may need to swap some A for B.
3. **Increase liquidity** on the existing position using the collected fees.

Compounding frequency: daily compounding adds marginal benefit over weekly compounding on most positions. The gas cost of more frequent transactions usually outweighs the compounding gain.

## Passive CLMM: wide ranges

If you want CLMM exposure without active management, use very wide ranges (±50–100% of current price). You get some capital efficiency benefit over CPMM without the rebalancing overhead.

Downside: capital efficiency is significantly lower than a well-managed tight range. For a truly passive approach, CPMM is simpler.

## Tracking performance

To evaluate whether your LP is performing well:

- **Compare to holding.** What would you hold if you weren't LPing? Calculate the holding value and compare to your LP value + collected fees.
- **Fee APR vs IL.** Are accumulated fees exceeding the IL you've experienced? If not, consider whether the pair is right for LP.
- **Time in range.** For CLMM, what fraction of the time has your position been in range? Anything below 60–70% on a position you claimed was active management suggests you're not monitoring enough.

## Exit criteria

Consider exiting a position when:

- The pair's volume has dropped and fee income no longer justifies IL exposure.
- One of the tokens has become risky (rug signs, project failure).
- A better opportunity is available and capital is tied up in a low-performing position.
- You need the capital for other use.

LP positions can be exited at any time. There is no lock-up unless you used [Burn & Earn](/user-flows/burn-and-earn).
