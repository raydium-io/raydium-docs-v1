<Info>
  If you want to deposit and forget, use **CPMM**. If you want to maximise fee income and can actively manage a price range, use **CLMM**. Both are live on Raydium and coexist for the same pairs.
</Info>

## How each model prices liquidity

### CPMM (constant-product)

CPMM uses the formula `x × y = k`. The pool holds reserves of token A and token B, and their product must always equal a constant. Price is just the ratio of the two reserves.

Your deposit goes into the full range — from price zero to infinity — meaning your capital is available for trades at any price. This is simple and never goes "out of range," but it also means most of your capital is sitting at prices far from the current price, earning nothing from real trades.

### CLMM (concentrated liquidity)

CLMM uses the same xy=k math locally, but lets you specify a **price range** `[lower, upper]` where your capital is active. Inside that range, your liquidity looks to a trader like a much larger CPMM pool. Outside the range, your liquidity earns nothing.

This is the Uniswap v3 model. A 10× capital efficiency gain means \$10k concentrated near the current price can match the depth of \$100k in a CPMM pool — but only while the price stays in range.

Raydium also runs a third, legacy program — **AMM v4**, a constant-product pool with an inactive OpenBook integration. It still trades, but new pools should use CPMM or CLMM; see [`products/amm-v4/overview`](/products/amm-v4/overview).

## Key differences

| | CPMM | CLMM |
|---|---|---|
| **Price range** | Full (0 → ∞) | User-specified `[lower, upper]` |
| **Capital efficiency** | Baseline | 5–100× higher when in range |
| **Fee accrual** | Auto-compounds into reserves | Accumulates as claimable balance |
| **Position token** | Fungible LP token | Non-fungible position NFT |
| **Out-of-range state** | Doesn't exist | Earns zero fees outside range |
| **Impermanent loss profile** | Standard full-range | Amplified within range, flat outside |
| **Rebalancing** | Not required | Required when price drifts to edge |
| **Best for** | Passive LPs, long-tail pairs | Active LPs, correlated or stable pairs |

## Capital efficiency: what it actually means

If the SOL/USDC price is \$160 and you expect it to trade between \$140–\$180 over the next month:

- A CPMM deposit of \$10,000 spreads capital from \$0 to ∞. Only a small fraction near \$160 is doing work.
- A CLMM deposit of \$10,000 in the \$140–\$180 range has all \$10k active. It provides the same trading depth as roughly \$80k in CPMM for that price band.

You earn fees proportional to your share of the active liquidity. At 20× concentration, your \$10k earns what \$200k earns in CPMM — while price stays in range.

## Fee accrual

**CPMM:** Fees are added to pool reserves continuously. Your LP token's redemption value grows. You don't need to claim — when you withdraw, you get your principal plus all accumulated fees.

**CLMM:** Fees accumulate as a separate pending balance on your position NFT. They must be collected explicitly. They do not compound automatically. Collect them when the gas cost is justified.

## The three questions that actually matter

**1. Am I willing to rebalance?** CLMM rewards active management; CPMM rewards inaction. If you can check your position weekly and move the range when price drifts to an edge, CLMM's fee capture per dollar is 4–40× higher than CPMM on the same pair and tier. If you can't, a CLMM position will silently sit out of range while CPMM LPs keep earning — choose CPMM.

**2. How correlated are the two tokens?** Impermanent loss grows with the divergence between the two prices. For tightly correlated assets (stable/stable, LST/SOL) IL is tiny and a narrow CLMM range is very effective. For weakly correlated assets (memecoin/SOL) IL can exceed a full year of fee income — widen the range or use CPMM. See [LP risks](/user-flows/lp-risks) and [impermanent loss](/algorithms/impermanent-loss).

**3. What fee tier matches the pair's volatility?** Higher volatility supports a higher fee tier, but too high a fee discourages volume and can net less revenue. Match the tier to how much the pair actually moves — see [How pool fees work](/user-flows/dynamic-fees).

## Decision matrix

| Your situation | Example pool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| First-time LP, want low ongoing effort | CPMM 0.25% | Single deposit, no range to manage, fees auto-compound in reserves. |
| Stable-to-stable (USDC/USDT, wSOL/stSOL) | CLMM 0.01% tight range | Tight range captures most fees at minimal IL; large capital efficiency vs CPMM. |
| Correlated but drifting (SOL/jitoSOL, bridged ETH) | CLMM 0.05% wide range | Captures fees, tolerates drift, still far more efficient than CPMM. |
| Volatile majors (SOL/USDC, BTC/USDC) | CLMM 0.25% medium range, or CPMM 0.25% | CLMM if you'll manage the range; CPMM if you'll set-and-forget. |
| Long-tail / memecoin or low-liquidity | CPMM 1% | Wide CLMM ranges barely beat CPMM here; CPMM's simpler accounting wins. |
| Need orderbook liquidity on top of the AMM | AMM v4 | The only program that integrates with OpenBook's orderbook. |
| Launching a new token bonding curve | LaunchLab (graduates to CPMM) | Not really a pool-type choice — LaunchLab picks CPMM for you. |

## Worked example: SOL/USDC as an LP

Assume \$10,000 of capital, SOL at \$160, and roughly \$150M of 30-day volume on each pool. Illustrative only:

| Pool | Your position | Fee APR* | Rebalance effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPMM 0.25% | \$10k in a \$50M pool | ~27% | None |
| CLMM 0.25%, ±5% range | \$10k concentrated | ~110% (in range) | Check weekly, rebalance on ~±4% drift |
| CLMM 0.05%, ±2% range | \$10k concentrated | ~180% (in range) | Check daily |
| AMM v4 0.25% | \$10k in a \$30M pool | ~18% | None |

\*APR is illustrative — real numbers depend on volume, realised volatility, your time-in-range, and compounding. Use [`algorithms/clmm-apr`](/algorithms/clmm-apr) to estimate for your parameters.

## Common mistakes

- **"CLMM always earns more."** Only while in range. Out-of-range CLMM earns zero; CPMM keeps earning at any price.
- **"Tighter range = more fees."** Yes while in range — and more frequent out-of-range events if you don't rebalance. Tight ranges on volatile pairs are for active managers.
- **"Higher fee tier = more fee income."** Not if the tier is too high for the pair and kills volume. Always check volume-to-TVL at each tier before choosing.
- **"LP token and position NFT are interchangeable."** CPMM LP tokens are fungible SPL tokens (farmable). CLMM position NFTs are non-fungible and carry your specific range. Farms stake CPMM / AMM v4 LP tokens, not CLMM position NFTs.
