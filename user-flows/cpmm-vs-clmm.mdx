<Info>
  If you want to deposit and forget, use **CPMM**. If you want to maximise fee income and can actively manage a price range, use **CLMM**. Both are live on Raydium and coexist for the same pairs.
</Info>

## How each model prices liquidity

### CPMM (constant-product)

CPMM uses the formula `x × y = k`. The pool holds reserves of token A and token B, and their product must always equal a constant. Price is just the ratio of the two reserves.

Your deposit goes into the full range — from price zero to infinity — meaning your capital is available for trades at any price. This is simple and never goes "out of range," but it also means most of your capital is sitting at prices far from the current price, earning nothing from real trades.

### CLMM (concentrated liquidity)

CLMM uses the same xy=k math locally, but lets you specify a **price range** `[lower, upper]` where your capital is active. Inside that range, your liquidity looks to a trader like a much larger CPMM pool. Outside the range, your liquidity earns nothing.

This is the Uniswap v3 model. A 10× capital efficiency gain means \$10k concentrated near the current price can match the depth of \$100k in a CPMM pool — but only while the price stays in range.

## Key differences

| | CPMM | CLMM |
|---|---|---|
| **Price range** | Full (0 → ∞) | User-specified `[lower, upper]` |
| **Capital efficiency** | Baseline | 5–100× higher when in range |
| **Fee accrual** | Auto-compounds into reserves | Accumulates as claimable balance |
| **Position token** | Fungible LP token | Non-fungible position NFT |
| **Out-of-range state** | Doesn't exist | Earns zero fees outside range |
| **IL profile** | Standard full-range IL | Amplified within range, flat outside |
| **Rebalancing** | Not required | Required when price drifts to edge |
| **Best for** | Passive LPs, long-tail pairs | Active LPs, correlated or stable pairs |

## Capital efficiency: what it actually means

If the SOL/USDC price is $160 and you expect it to trade between $140–$180 over the next month:

- A CPMM deposit of \$10,000 spreads capital from $0 to ∞. Only a small fraction near $160 is doing work.
- A CLMM deposit of \$10,000 in the $140–$180 range has all \$10k active. It provides the same trading depth as roughly \$80k in CPMM for that price band.

You earn fees proportional to your share of the active liquidity. At 20× concentration, your \$10k earns what \$200k earns in CPMM — while price stays in range.

## IL in CPMM vs CLMM

CPMM IL is the standard formula and is well understood. See [impermanent loss math](/algorithms/impermanent-loss).

CLMM IL is **amplified** within the range. For the same price move, you lose more relative to holding than you would in CPMM, because your liquidity is concentrated and doing more work. But if price exits the range, your IL stops changing — the position becomes 100% one token and doesn't worsen further.

Net: CLMM earns more fees and suffers more IL when in range. CPMM earns less but the IL grows more slowly.

## Fee accrual

**CPMM:** Fees are added to pool reserves continuously. Your LP token's redemption value grows. You don't need to claim — when you withdraw, you get your principal plus all accumulated fees.

**CLMM:** Fees accumulate as a separate pending balance on your position NFT. They must be collected explicitly. They do not compound automatically. Collect them when the gas cost is justified.

## The management question

The real deciding factor is how much time you are willing to spend.

- If you check positions **less than once a week**: CPMM. CLMM ranges need monitoring, especially on volatile pairs.
- If you check positions **weekly and will rebalance**: CLMM with a medium range (±15–30% around current price). Large efficiency gains, moderate management load.
- If you check positions **daily**: CLMM tight range (±5–10%). Maximum fee capture, high rebalance frequency.

An unmonitored CLMM position in a volatile pair can spend most of its time out of range, earning nothing while CPMM LPs in the same pool keep earning. That is the most common CLMM mistake.

## Which pairs suit each pool

| Pair type | Better pool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Stablecoin/stablecoin | CLMM 0.01% tight | Near-zero IL, high concentration profitable |
| LST/SOL (jitoSOL, mSOL) | CLMM 0.05% medium | Correlated, moderate drift |
| SOL/USDC | Either | CLMM active for higher APR; CPMM passive |
| Long-tail / memecoin | CPMM 1% | Extreme volatility makes tight CLMM impractical |
| New token launch | CPMM | Uncertainty in price distribution |
