<Info>
  Use **CPMM** if you want to deposit and forget. Use **CLMM** if you want to maximise fee income and can actively manage a price range. Both are live on Raydium and coexist for the same pairs — the right choice comes down to how much you'll manage the position and how correlated the two tokens are.
</Info>

## How each model works

Both pools price trades with the same constant-product math; the difference is *where* your capital sits.

### CPMM (constant-product)

A CPMM pool holds reserves of token A and token B and keeps their product constant: `x × y = k`. The price is simply the ratio of the two reserves, and your deposit spreads across the **full price range**, from zero to infinity.

That makes it effortless — your liquidity is available at any price and can never go "out of range" — but it also means most of your capital sits at prices far from the current one, doing nothing. Fees accrue automatically into the reserves, so your LP token's value grows without you claiming anything.

### CLMM (concentrated liquidity)

A CLMM pool uses the same `x × y = k` math, but only within a **price range** `[lower, upper]` that you choose. Inside that range your capital behaves like a much larger CPMM pool, so you earn far more fees per dollar; outside it, your liquidity is entirely in one token and earns nothing.

This is the Uniswap v3 model. The upside is large — concentrating capital can multiply fee income many times over — but it requires you to keep the price inside your range, which means rebalancing as the market moves. Fees here don't compound; they accumulate as a claimable balance on your position NFT, which you collect when it's worth the gas.

<Note>
  Raydium also runs a third, legacy program — **AMM v4**, a constant-product pool (OpenBook integration deactivated). It still trades, but new pools should use CPMM or CLMM. See [`products/amm-v4/overview`](/products/amm-v4/overview).
</Note>

## Side-by-side

| | CPMM | CLMM |
|---|---|---|
| **Price range** | Full (0 → ∞) | User-specified `[lower, upper]` |
| **Capital efficiency** | Baseline | 5–100× higher while in range |
| **Fee accrual** | Auto-compounds into reserves | Claimable balance on the position |
| **Position token** | Fungible LP token | Non-fungible position NFT |
| **Out-of-range state** | Doesn't exist | Earns zero fees outside range |
| **Impermanent loss** | Standard full-range | Amplified within range, flat outside |
| **Rebalancing** | Not required | Required when price drifts to an edge |
| **Best for** | Passive LPs, long-tail pairs | Active LPs, correlated or stable pairs |

## Capital efficiency: what it actually buys you

This is the whole reason CLMM exists, so it's worth making concrete. Say SOL/USDC trades at \$160 and you expect it to stay between \$140 and \$180 over the next month:

- A **CPMM** deposit of \$10,000 spreads from \$0 to ∞. Only the sliver of capital near \$160 is doing real work; the rest is idle.
- A **CLMM** deposit of the same \$10,000, concentrated in the \$140–\$180 band, has all of it active. It provides roughly the same trading depth a \$80,000 CPMM position would.

Because you earn fees in proportion to your share of the *active* liquidity, that concentration translates directly into more fees per dollar — at 20× concentration your \$10k earns what \$200k would earn in CPMM. The catch is the same in reverse: that multiplier only applies **while the price is in your range**, and the tighter the range, the more often the price leaves it.

## How to choose

Three questions settle it for almost every pair:

**1. Will you rebalance?** CLMM rewards active management; CPMM rewards inaction. If you can check the position weekly and move the range when price drifts to an edge, CLMM's fee capture per dollar is several times CPMM's on the same pair and tier. If you can't, a CLMM position will silently sit out of range while CPMM LPs keep earning — choose CPMM.

**2. How correlated are the two tokens?** IL grows with how far the two prices diverge. For tightly correlated assets (stable/stable, LST/SOL), IL is tiny and a narrow CLMM range is extremely effective. For weakly correlated assets (memecoin/SOL), IL can exceed a full year of fees — widen the range or use CPMM. See [LP risks](/user-flows/lp-risks) and [impermanent loss](/algorithms/impermanent-loss).

**3. What fee tier matches the pair's volatility?** A more volatile pair supports a higher fee tier, but too high a fee discourages volume and can net less revenue. Match the tier to how much the pair actually moves — see [How pool fees work](/user-flows/dynamic-fees).

## Which to pick

| Your situation | Example pool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| First-time LP, want low ongoing effort | CPMM 0.25% | Single deposit, no range to manage, fees auto-compound. |
| Stable-to-stable (USDC/USDT, wSOL/stSOL) | CLMM 0.01% tight range | Captures most fees at minimal IL; large efficiency gain over CPMM. |
| Correlated but drifting (SOL/jitoSOL, bridged ETH) | CLMM 0.05% wide range | Captures fees, tolerates drift, still far more efficient than CPMM. |
| Volatile majors (SOL/USDC, BTC/USDC) | CLMM 0.25% medium range, or CPMM 0.25% | CLMM if you'll manage the range; CPMM if you'll set-and-forget. |
| Long-tail / memecoin or low-liquidity | CPMM 1% | Wide CLMM ranges barely beat CPMM here; CPMM's simpler accounting wins. |
| Launching a new token bonding curve | LaunchLab (graduates to CPMM) | Not a pool-type choice — LaunchLab picks CPMM for you. |

## Worked example: SOL/USDC as an LP

Assume \$10,000 of capital, SOL at \$160, and roughly \$150M of 30-day volume on each pool. Illustrative only — real numbers depend on volume, realised volatility, your time-in-range, and compounding:

| Pool | Your position | Fee APR\* | Rebalance effort |
|---|---|---|---|
| CPMM 0.25% | \$10k in a \$50M pool | ~27% | None |
| CLMM 0.25%, ±5% range | \$10k concentrated | ~110% (in range) | Check weekly, rebalance on ~±4% drift |
| CLMM 0.05%, ±2% range | \$10k concentrated | ~180% (in range) | Check daily |
| AMM v4 0.25% | \$10k in a \$30M pool | ~18% | None |

\*Use [`algorithms/clmm-apr`](/algorithms/clmm-apr) to estimate APR for your own parameters.

The pattern is the standard CLMM trade-off: the tighter you concentrate, the higher the in-range APR and the more often you have to rebalance to stay there.

## Common mistakes

- **"CLMM always earns more."** Only while in range. Out-of-range CLMM earns zero; CPMM keeps earning at any price.
- **"Tighter range = more fees."** True while in range — and it also means more frequent out-of-range events if you don't rebalance. Tight ranges on volatile pairs are for active managers only.
- **"Higher fee tier = more income."** Not if the tier is too high for the pair and kills volume. Always check volume-to-TVL at each tier before choosing.
- **"LP tokens and position NFTs are interchangeable."** CPMM LP tokens are fungible and farmable; CLMM position NFTs are non-fungible and carry your specific range. Farms stake CPMM / AMM v4 LP tokens, not CLMM position NFTs.
